# Market map (directional research notes, this session)

Categories adjacent to FlowLens, and how each falls short of "process intelligence":

- **Native OS tools**: Windows Steps Recorder (deprecated by Microsoft; left a real gap for
  Windows-based IT/support teams who relied on it for lightweight reproduction steps).
- **Screen-recording/async video**: Loom, ClickUp Clips — great for communication, poor for
  structured, searchable, actionable evidence.
- **Step-by-step guide generators**: Scribe, Tango, Guidde, Supademo — strong at turning a
  capture into a how-to doc; none diagnose friction or route to tickets/audit packs.
- **Bug/feedback capture**: Jam.dev, Marker.io, Ybug — narrow to QA/bug workflows, not
  positioned for ops/compliance/training use cases.
- **SOP/process/knowledge enforcement**: Process Street, Trainual, Whatfix, WalkMe — strong
  once a process is written down; none generate the process from observed real work.
- **General screenshot/capture utility**: ShareX, Snagit — power-user tools, no AI layer, no
  team workflow.

FlowLens's whitespace: nobody combines capture + AI diagnosis + multi-format routing + ROI
measurement in one loop. This is treated as a durable, defensible category position rather
than a feature gap any single competitor is likely to close quickly.
